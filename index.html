Everyone knows that government spying is out of control. Chelsea Manning has a real proposal to address it.

Right now, politicians are making decisions that will affect the future of our freedom of expression and basic human rights. The U.S. government's unpopular mass surveillance programs have been heavily criticized, but so far Congress has failed to meaningfully curtail them, and is even moving to expand them through legislation like the Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act of 2015 (CISA.)

What the world needs right now is for smart people who actually understand how these programs work to chime in and offer real solutions for how to rein them in.

Chelsea Manning, the Wikileaks whistleblower who exposed some of the U.S. government's worst abuses, was an Army intelligence analyst before she was locked away with an unjust 35 year sentence. She understands how these programs work and has a unique perspective as someone who gets how the actual people who use this data think.

Chelsea has written a 129 page surveillance reform bill that EVERYONE should read, but most importantly we should encourage the members of Congress who are supposed to represent us to read it and take it into consideration as they move to address these issues. The bill starts by abolishing the secretive and controversial Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court, and it also includes a proposed alternative to CISA.

Check out Chelsea's column in The Guardian about the bill, and make sure to sign the petition asking your lawmakers to read it. They might learn something!

Chelsea Manning, the Guardian columnist and whistleblower responsible for one of WikiLeaks most important releases, has been in jail for five years facing an unjust 35 year sentence for exposing some of the U.S. governments worst abuses.

Now, Chelsea is being threatened with indefinite solitary confinement, and the "charges" against her demonstrate that her basic human rights are being violated. Chelsea requested that her hearing be made open to the public, but officials refused.

BREAKING UPDATE: With just two days left before she has to present her defense in front the disciplinary board, prison staff are now denying Chelsea access to the facility's legal library.

Chelsea's alleged "crimes" include: sweeping some food onto the floor and then asking to speak to her lawyer when a guard confronted her, having books and magazines in her cell about politics and LGBTQ issues including the Vanity Fair issue with Caitlyn Jenner on the cover, and "improper medicine use" for having a tube of toothpaste that was past its expiration date.

It couldn't be more blatant: Chelsea is being singled out and punished for speaking out.

Chelsea is a human being. She did something brave and now the government is punishing her for it. It's not just Chelsea's basic rights that are at stake, it's all of ours.
